When is the best time to fly if you want to avoid flight delays?

Does the time of flight affect the chances of it being delayed? Is there a preferred day or month to fly if you want to minimize the chances of your flight being delayed?

Source of Data

Data was gathered from the Department of Transportation's web site On-Time Performances Table, between November 2016 and October 2017 (inclusive).

The data was processed in order to create a histogram of late flights by different time periods. Delay is considered when a flight arrived more than 15 minutes after its planned time. The data includes all domestic flights and delays for all reasons (security, weather, etc.)

For any available graph (day of week, day of month, and month of year) the percentage of delayed flights is shown. The category with the most delays is marked with a red bar, while the category with the least delays is marked with a green bar.

Insights

  • If you plan to fly on Friday, the chances of your flight being delayed are 5%(!) higher than when flying on Saturday.
  • The 7th day of every month has the highest chance of having a delayed flight (over 21%).
  • December is the worst month to fly if you want to avoid flight delays. The chances of your flight being delayed in November are almost 10% less than in December.
